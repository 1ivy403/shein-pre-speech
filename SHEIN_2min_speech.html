PIA5058 - 2分钟口语稿

主题：4.1 环境分析（全生命周期） | 风格：雅思 6.5 水平，简单清晰

主稿（约 2 分钟，升级逻辑版）

[Slide 1 - 开场：问题与框架] Good morning everyone. Our question is simple: can ultra-fast fashion really be sustainable? My answer uses one clear logic: we must evaluate SHEIN across the whole life cycle, not only one step. So I will follow four links: raw materials, production, logistics, and end-of-life. My core claim is: reducing inventory waste is progress, but it is not equal to reducing total environmental impact. [Slide 2 - 核心链条分析] First, raw materials. SHEIN still relies heavily on polyester, around 82%. Polyester is fossil-fuel based and hard to degrade, so the system starts with a high-carbon foundation. Second, production. SHEIN's on-demand small-batch model can reduce unsold stock, and this is a real efficiency gain. But sustainability is about total impact. In 2024, Scope 3 emissions still increased by 23.1%. So the growth of total volume is faster than efficiency improvement. Third, logistics. SHEIN ships many small parcels across countries. This gives consumers speed and convenience, but it also creates high transport emissions. So in this model, fast delivery and low carbon are still in tension. Fourth, end-of-life. Many items are low-price and short-use, so disposal happens quickly. Synthetic fabrics also release microplastics during washing and after disposal. This means environmental pressure does not stop at checkout; it continues after use. [Slide 3 - 收束：结论与启示] To conclude, SHEIN shows operational improvements, but the life-cycle pressure is still high. The key contradiction is structural: if volume keeps rising, total impact will keep rising. So real sustainability needs not only efficiency, but also slower overproduction, cleaner materials, and stronger rules. Thank you.

30秒备用版（节点页码版）

[Slide 1] My logic is full life cycle: raw materials, production, logistics, and end-of-life. [Slide 2] SHEIN has efficiency progress, especially in reducing inventory waste. But the key issue is total scale. In 2024, Scope 3 emissions still rose by 23.1%. So less waste per item is not equal to less total environmental impact. [Slide 3] Real sustainability needs both efficiency and structural change in volume, materials, and governance.
