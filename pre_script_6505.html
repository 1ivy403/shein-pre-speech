Title — What is Shadow Price
~30 sec

OK, so my part focuses on one question: how does "shadow price" actually influence corporate investment?

First, what is shadow price? [pause] It is not the carbon cost companies pay today. It is their prediction of what carbon will cost them in the future — including compliance spending, buying quotas, or internal pricing they set themselves.

The real concern is: will my long-term cash flow survive if policy keeps tightening? Will my assets become stranded? [pause] That prediction shapes where money goes.

我这部分聚焦一个问题：影子价格到底怎么影响企业投资？

首先，影子价格不是企业今天交的碳费，而是企业对未来碳成本的预测——合规支出、配额购买、内部碳价。核心关注是：政策继续收紧，我的现金流还能撑住吗？资产会不会搁浅？这个预判决定了钱往哪儿走。

2
CPU — The Policy Uncertainty Shock
~45 sec

But here is the problem: policy signals are not always clear.

Gavriilidis in 2026 built a Climate Policy Uncertainty index using news data. He found that when uncertainty rises, it works like a supply shock — output falls, prices rise, and companies with high climate exposure cut investment and R&D.

Liu, also in 2026, looked at the EU carbon market and found something interesting: [pause] in a trading market, trading behavior itself amplifies price swings. So the carbon price companies actually feel can be very different from what the policy document says.

→ Key takeaway: when policy is unclear, companies discount more heavily. Their internal shadow price disconnects from the official path, and investment response weakens.

但问题在于政策信号并不总是清晰的。Gavriilidis（2026）用新闻文本构建了"气候政策不确定性指数"，发现不确定性上升就像供给冲击——产出降、价格涨，高气候暴露的企业砍投资和研发。Liu（2026）在欧盟碳市场发现：交易行为本身会放大碳价波动，企业感受到的价格和政策文件上的不一样。所以关键是：政策越模糊，企业折现越保守，影子价格和官方路径脱节，投资反应就弱了。

3
Canada Carbon Price Path
~40 sec

Now let's look at a concrete example. [pause] Canada's federal benchmark sets a clear schedule: the carbon price rises by 15 Canadian dollars per tonne every year, reaching 170 dollars per tonne by 2030.

For companies, this schedule is like a price escalator — it gives them a basis to set their shadow price.

However, [pause] in March 2025, the government updated the benchmark page. The focus shifted to industrial emissions pricing, and the consumer-side fuel charge was removed. [pause] So the old path and the new signal now coexist. If policy direction shifts, companies need to reassess their carbon cost assumptions.

来看具体案例。加拿大联邦基准设定了清晰的涨价表：每年涨15加元/吨，到2030年达170加元/吨。企业可以据此设影子价格。但2025年3月基准页更新，重心转向工业排放定价，消费端燃料费取消了。旧路径和新信号并存，如果方向不一致，企业就得重新评估碳成本假设。

4
Internal Channel — ICP
~35 sec

Even when the external carbon price is clear, what happens inside the company matters too.

ICP — Internal Carbon Pricing — is a management tool. Companies write carbon cost into their project approval process.

But Qin in 2023 found that [pause] ICP does not push all types of investment equally. Energy intensity plays a positive role — meaning energy-heavy projects respond well. But R&D spending actually has a shielding effect — it absorbs the pressure, so less money flows into other areas.

→ In short: shadow price enters the budget, but how money is distributed inside the company can be quite uneven.

即使外部碳价清晰，企业内部怎么处理也很关键。ICP（内部碳定价）是企业把碳成本写进项目审批的管理工具。但Qin（2023）发现ICP不会均匀推动所有类型的投资——能源密度起正向中介作用，而研发支出有"遮蔽效应"，会吸收压力。所以影子价格进了预算，但钱怎么分配，内部差异很大。

5
Sector & International Level
~40 sec (Slides 5–6)

Now, does shadow price work the same across all industries and countries? [pause] No.

Dilek in 2026 studied California and found that when carbon pricing works together with electricity market rules, renewable energy investment actually increases. So under the right system, carbon price can redirect money.

But for hard-to-reduce industries — like steel or cement — the IEA points out that carbon price alone is not enough. You also need subsidies, long-term contracts, and technical standards, especially for things like CCUS.

And Bistline in 2023 showed that in the US, capital decisions are shaped by multiple factors — tax credits, electricity prices, interest rates — not just carbon price. [pause] So we should avoid explaining investment changes by carbon price alone.

影子价格在所有行业和国家都一样管用吗？不是。Dilek（2026）在加州发现，碳定价和电力市场规则配合时，可再生能源投资确实增加了。但IEA指出，难减排行业（如钢铁、水泥）单靠碳价不够，还需要补贴、长约和技术标准。Bistline（2023）表明美国的资本配置受税收抵免、电价、利率等多重因素影响，不能只用碳价来解释。

(continued — cross-border context)

Globally, carbon taxes and emissions trading systems are still spreading. [pause] Placing the Canadian case in this bigger picture helps us avoid isolated interpretation — what happens in Canada is connected to global trends.

全球范围内碳税和碳交易体系仍在扩散，把加拿大案例放在国际大背景下看，避免孤立解读。

7
Conclusion
~25 sec

So, to wrap up. [pause] Whether a rising carbon price can actually turn into low-carbon investment depends on five things:

One, is the policy signal stable? [brief] Two, does the carbon market add uncertainty? [brief] Three, does the company's internal pricing match its actual investment channels? [brief] Four, does the industry need tools beyond carbon pricing? [brief] And five, do neighboring countries' policies change the picture?

The bottom line is: carbon price guides investment, but the process is not automatic, not linear, and not frictionless.

That's my part. Thank you.

总结：碳价上涨能否真正转化为低碳投资，取决于五个条件——政策信号稳定性、碳市场不确定性、企业内部ICP与实际投资渠道是否匹配、行业是否需要碳价以外的配套工具、邻国政策与要素价格。碳价引导投资，但这个过程不是自动的、线性的、无摩擦的。以上是我的部分，谢谢。
